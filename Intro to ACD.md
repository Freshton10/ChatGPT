### Intro to ACD/IVR

Automatic Contact Distributor (ACD) allows you to configure general settings that support your interaction channels, your agents, and the way you route between the two information about skills, dispositions, points of contact, and quick replies.
An Interactive Voice Response (IVR) is an automated phone system that allows incoming callers to bypass live agents by accessing information via a voice response system of pre-recorded messages. An IVR uses touch tone keypad menus or speech recognition to route calls to specific departments or specialists. 
ACD/IVR retrieves data, such as call logs, response logs from scripts, or dispositions, using RingCentralâ€™s Application Programming Interfaces (APIs).

**How do I set up ACD/IVR?**
Contact RingCentral Support to set up and/IVR for your company. Your support agent may set up sessions with you to discuss  ACD/IVR flow implementation and required documentation.
What do I need to know about ACD/IVR flow implementation?
Your support agent may discuss the metrics that your company needs, the types of reporting available to you, and different options for optimizing caller experience. They will also discuss Studio and Visio flowcharting with you. Your agent may also ask you about:
Hours of operation for groups
Skills and service levels (SLAs) 
Points of Contact (POCs)

**What do I need to know about the required documentation for ACD/IVR?**
You may need to provide the following documents to implement ACD/IVR:
Requirements Approval Email (RAE): Provides finalized requirements documentation to customers during the implementation process.
Data Collection Workbook: The Data Collection Workbook is where requirements for Central/User Hub and Integration are documented. The Data Collection Workbook is designed to allow you to use prebuilt APIs to upload data automatically to Central/Userhub.
Visio Call Flow Templates: The designs that you can use when you create your IVR call flows. You can use these templates as starting points for creating the pre-requirement call flows that you'll present to the customer in your first requirements session.
